History

Now the history of Rome is pretty extensive, to say the least. But to sum it up, Rome has three periods: the Period of Kings, Republican Rome, and Imperial Rome.

Period of Kings (625-510 BC)

Legend has it that Rome was founded by Romulus and Remus, twin sons of the god of war, Mars. After killing his brother, Romulus took the title as the first king of Rome, thereby starting the Roman Period of Kings. This period had only six kings after Romulus, under which Rome saw many militaristic and economic advances. It was in this period that the Roman constitution was formed, paving the way for the Republican period of Rome.

Republican Rome (510-31 BC)

Rome is no longer under the rule of a king, and developed a new form of government: a Republic. Governemnt matters were now settled by the upper class, primarily senators. Yet the Romans would elect a dictator in times of crisis. Rome took control of the entire Italian peninsula in this period and became the dominant Mediterranean power. Civil war followed soon after leaving in a state of unrest the led to the rise of dictator Julius Caesar. Under Caesar Rome became increasingly imperialistic, extending its borders beyond the Mediterranean. Caesar's assassination in 44 BC led to the rise of Octavian as the first emperor of Rome.

Imperial Rome (31 BC - AD 476)

This final period of Rome lasted from the rise of its first emperor to the Rome's fall. Rome was in a good period of its life during this time. The empire had reached its maximum size, spanning across three continents including Asia Minor, Northern Africa, and most of Europe. The empire had grown so large that in AD 286 it split itself into two united empires, each with its own emperor. Unfortunately, the western empire fell in AD 455 upon suffering Gothic invasions. This is largely considered the end of the overall Roman empire, however the eastern Roman empire (aka the Byzantine Empire) survived until the 15th century, when Turks took control of the capital city, Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul).
